Variation on Emperor's new clothes:

Instead of emperor or more modern day 'famous' person.
Conversation of Tipi with someone. 
Story about emperor who is fond of finest things, then gets cheated by ppl claiming to make the most excuisite items that only those worthy could see.
"I know how this goes. In the end, a child points out that which adults do not: that the emperor is not wearing any clothings."
Real lesson:
The child's perspective is simple and unaffected by clouded societal teachings. After that the adults also dare to laugh and see it for what it is and the emperor has been taught a lesson in humility. Also the lackies that played along are exposed as bootlicking servants. And the imposters as common thieves. But this way the lesson is lost. Tipi remains the claim that the emperor is wearing layers and layers of clothing (burdens of life and past experiences) and deserves not to be mocked, as we are all fools and this shows emperors can be fools too. The adults are showing the wounds of childhood and regress to childlike behavior; gleefull and judgmental about the 'haves' elite. While failing to acknowledge that everyone is deserving of empathy, and the imposters owning the real truth. We should treat everyone as if they are clothed, even if its invisible or not there.
