interpretation: Tomi is an eager student. So eager that he wants results before he understands them. Whenever we define our philosophy in any way, we must realize that we merely construct a system that supports what we already want to understand. Tomi should not be surprised to find a philosophy if he merely follows the path of finding one, just as he should not be surprised to find the Toki Ponist by following the path to his cabin. Tomi leaves relieved and starts his search by getting lost, instead of finding.

`jan Tomi` rushes up the mountain. He thrusts the door open. `jan Sipi o`, he exclaims to the Toki Ponist, 'I have found my philosophy!'
The Toki Ponist asks: 'How did you find me, to tell me this?'. 'Simple,' Tomi replied, 'I followed the path up the mountain to your cabin'. The Toki Ponist retorts, 'Then why do you act so surprised about what you have found?' and he closes the door in Tomi's face. `jan Tomi li pona`, stumbles down the mountain and gets lost on the way. -->
