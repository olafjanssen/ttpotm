The story on how I became **The Toki Ponist on the Mountain** is not a simple one. It deals with time travel, other realities and inner struggles turned inside out. It will be told in a novel that is taking more and more form.

<img src="/img/self-portrait-01.png">

In order to be what I am, my life can not be what it is. You may wonder why my beard does not look the part. Well, it takes time to grow a beard worthy of convincing people that I am a guru. The beard may never reach that length, because time works strange on this mountain.

Oh yes, the mountain is essential for being **The Toki Ponist on the Mountain**. What does this mountain look like and where can you find it?

<img src="/img/mountain-view-01.png">

It sort of looks like this and it is situated in an area that more or less looks like this. You must understand that the intention of being on a mountain is not to be found. However, there is a path of course that ends up at my cabin dwelling. But there is no telling where the other end leads to.

<img src="/img/cabin-view-01.png">

On top of the mountain it looks a bit like this. There is my wooden log cabin with a simple tool shed. A fireplace nearby for cooking, a clear spring that magically appears there and some trees and bushes to complete the mess. A trail Fibonaccis away from the cabin down the mountain. Like I said, I do not know where it leads because I never take the trail.
