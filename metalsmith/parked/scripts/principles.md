## Problems and Solving

What do we do when we find ourselves in a flurry of problems? We try to solve those problems. 

<!-- That's all fine and peachy. But what does that even mean? -->

That annoying neighbour, that knot in your shoelaces that won't come out, that reprimandable math assignment. These problems form obstacles and we want them gone, out of our lives. 

<!-- Poof. -->

We like to call approaching a problem: solving it. So let's problemize a cube of sugar. Just put it in water and it disappears. Voila! We have created a solution. Problem solved. 

Of course the sugar isn't really gone. It's only tranformed into something else. The sugar appears to have disappeared because it is hidden between the molecules of the solvent, water. However, it is still there and we know it because the water is now sweet.

And so by radical extension. I give you three governing principles that hold for any problem and its solutions:

1. problems don't go away, ever, even if you solve them
2. a problem is always part of the solution
3. a solvent is that which transforms the problem

So what do we do? We keep noodling the appearence of the problem until we are less annoyed by it in the short term. We yell at our neighbour until she cries, we cut our shoe laces with scissors and we tear our math assignment to pieces. Problems solved. Dandy.

## Philosophy and Idiots

When everybody and their pet duck assumes that a thing is utterly true. A philosopher will come by and postulate exactly the opposite, that it is false. This is why it is very hard to get rid of philosophers. When everyone agrees, they disagree. And when everybody disagrees, nobody notices the annoyed philsophers sulking in a corner.

It is a philosopher's job to make everyone else feel like an idiot. So how does a philisopher stand out? Because the craving to make others feel like idiots is universal.

Self-help writers, teachers, consultants, religious leaders and your average bvlogger also make people feel like idiots and we eat it up. Why? Because these charlatans after turning you into an idiot, give you the tool to be better and turn others into idiots. A blog post will read: "10 ways why you are shit and what to do about it", not "10 ways why you are shit and how I gloat about it".

A true philosopher will not give you this tool, he will dryly make you feel bad about your most personal beliefs. For the best effect you require more philosophers, to point out that whatever way you look at something, you are an idiot.

## Reality and Illusion
Fellow gurus often claim that many things that bother you are just an illusion, like time, the past or suffering, and that illusions are not real. These people are, of course, idiots.

An illusion is often described as a distorted experience of reality. But because every sense is distorted by our limited brain, everything once processed by us would be an illusion. Reality is the _objective what_ that we experience, no matter how we do it.

Principle: illusions are real, but not everything that is real, exists

Is time an illusion? To experience time you need mass. Light has no mass and experiences the entire universe as a single given. So it goes with the other particles, in essence they are massless and therefore timeless. But the interactions in the fabric of the universe let us experience something like mass, we call it something with a Higg's field to give the emergence a creator. Mass and time emerge from a universe without. It is real, but not does exist.

There are illusions, things that are just in our head. Sometimes we think a pill will cure us and then we get better, even though the pill was just a placebo. Now here is an illusion that exists

Some things are an illusion, such as time and mass. They emerge from a timeless and massless place. Still it is real to us. Allows a form of time-travel, later more on that. Real in your head: placebo, doesn't mean real for everyone.
