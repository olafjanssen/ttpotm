What do people mean when they want to solve a problem? It is an obstacle that they want to have disappeared. We then say we want to solve a problem. But by solving something it is not gone, it has only tranformed into something else. If you solve sugar in water, the sugar appears to have disappeared because it is hidden between the water molecules. However, it is still there and it can be perceived, albeit in a different form: the water has become sweet.

So what, in a general case, is the solvent in which you solve a problem?

Solving a mathematical problem means rearranging existing data by use of logic and deduction until a simple expression has been found. You transform the existing data and change its appearance until it has become something that makes you understand something else better. The solvent is therefore logic.

Also for other types of problems, we must carefully see what property is transformed into something else using a method or model so that the problematic property expresses itself differently. In such a way that we consider further transformation unnecessary.

Two things are therefore important:
1. the problem remains to exist and does not disappear
2. the problem is part of the solution, the solvent makes the transformation possible
