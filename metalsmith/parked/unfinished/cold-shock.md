We assume an earlier discussed connection between body and mind. The secret agreements between the two are not always transparent and sometimes pacts are made and deals are closed that are not beneficial to either of them. This is often not done on purpose or with full understanding. In such cases a ritual may help as neutral arbiter and councellor.

![cold-shock](/img/topic-coldshock.png)

One of the areas is about making the body resilient in a literal sense but also submitting yourself to something that cannot do real harm but acts like a mental reset.

Examples can be bunjee-jumping or sky-diving. To many this will be a natural brake to do this voluntarily and from their own volition. Still, many who do these things feel liberated afterwards. However, there are simpler and equally effective ways of achieving this. Often you are pressured by peers to do these things or the instructor has to give you the final push over the edge. It is better if it is done from your own initiative. In addition, the aforementioned activities are reasonbly safe when supervised by a trained expert, but the act itself is a form of simulated death, because without the expert it will lead to certain death. Therefore, it is an activity (not a real ritual, recurring) with its own purpose and use. The ritual discussed here is about transcending the mind of non-real fear of death on a mundane, daily level.

The cold shick is a good and well known manner to let your body undergo something that it should be perfectly capable of dealing with, but that may pose as an insurmountable problem for the mind.

Known is the idea that you have to enter a (cold) swimming pool: step by step you get used to the cold only to rush back to the warmth of a thick towel or warm shower.

Jump into the water, or set your shower towards the end on cold water only. This will initially give a shock. Before you start your mind will produce thoughts that you cannot do it, that it amounts to nothing, that you don't want to suffer needlessly, that you are doing something terrible to yourself, that you can try again tomorrow if you are less tired or more secure. At this point in time it is essential that the deal of the ritual is saved into the muscles and that intuitive the body will make the inititative to turn the knob to all cold, so that the conscious mind only can only watch and accept.

The body will feel the cold and get into a kind of shock. You will breathe fast and shallow, and the terrible cold may feel like pain. Your conscious will not think about daisies and dairy; the shock acts like a kind of reset. After this, the survival thoughts arise: I am dying, I am to flee, I have to turn off the water or turn up the heat. More or less at the same time the body will gain more control. This might take a while in the beginning and so you should train and build up slowly. Control means a relaxed breathing pattern and pulse, shrinking of certain body parts, and observing the cold without the sensation of pain. Conscious thoughts are still practical and goal oriented but are no longer aimed at pure survival. Subconsciously, you will learn to accept the situation and to appreciate it, in order to succumb to it better the next time.

A ritual connects the body with the mind and the conscious with the unconscious.

For instance with the cold shock:
* body + unconscious = muscle memory, intuitive decision power (turning the cold water knob)
* body + conscious = conscious observes and analyses the behavior of the body
* unconscious + conscious = conscious trains the unconscios by labelling the experience as safe (numbing)

* body trains warm-cold reflect and anti-bodies, controlling your breathing
* conscious gets a reset, the cold prevents unnecessary thoughts
* unconscious will update its relationship to the selves to deal with the new situation
 


It will be warm when you leave the shower and the rest of the day, you need less of the tower and therefore end feeling strong instead of in need of help (from a towel).
