Anger is a pain that wants to get out and will get out. Like a wild beast it gnaws on the jail bars of the body. Once in a while, a cry will escape and hit innocent bystanders. The anger does not disappear from this.

Something has to die so that something else can live. Something must break to keep the rest whole. That is the motivation that keeps the engine running, that keeps the monster inside gnawing. The anger comes when the running stops; it is the phase past fleeing and before dissociation.

But it is an internal fight and when there are no casualties outside, there will be casualties inside. A channel must be opened to safely work out the anger.

Physical exercise combined with loud outcries. But alone and in silence. This is the challenge. How do you prevent contained anger-sneezes. We need anger-sneezes without the danger of contamination.
