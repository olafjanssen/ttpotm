`jan ale li pona`

Our mind is always right, even if it is wrong, and that is a phenomenon that is hard to accept. Especially because this means that others are also right. Even if they claim the opposite of what you say or think.

Even though everyone is right not everyone will be proven right or be allowed to be proven right. Nor is every right worth equally. Your personal right depends on your level of development or world view. The world view on the next level automatically disagrees, while still being right, and the world view above that one might agree again with the first view but for completely different reasons.

Being proven right depends on the ruling world view; the value by the world view of society. The right of the highest world view should theoretically be the most valuable, but because of the ruling world view this is often not the case in practice. Being proven right, or forcing your right even if you are at a higher level op development will not work and be a fruitless battle.

The most important is not to emphasize the opinion or the being right in itself. Even the arguments should not emphasized, because they are also world view dependent. No. Every view needs something. Something fed from a fear, a miss or a fixation. Numb this fear and then their right no longer matters. Find out what someone needs and how to fulfill this need?

By supporting everyone in their right allows someone to grow to the next level. This is possible for every individual and thereby as a society as a whole.

* everyone is right
* remove opinion/judgement from the story
* unravel per world view what their existential need is
* fulfil this need
* choose a solution that fits with the upper end of the ruling world view or a level above it
