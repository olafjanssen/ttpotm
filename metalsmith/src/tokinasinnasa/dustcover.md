###### These are the words as recovered from the *awoken well*:

<koan>
jan Akesipu li tawa sewi e ma nena suli.  
ona li pana e lipu Pu tawa ma poka noka pi jan Sipi.  
ona li toki e ni:  
<li>mi lukin e lipu e ni.  
tenpo ni la, mi sina sama.</li>
jan Sipi li kama jo e lipu kepeken pilin pona.
ona li toki e ni: lon anu seme?
jan Akesipu li toki e ni: mi lukin e ni tawa sina.
jan Sipi li toki e ni: pona. tenpo open la, o moku e waso poka mi.
jan Akesipu li moku e waso poka jan Sipi.
ona li pini la ona tawa sewi lon weka pi ma nena suli.
ona li tawa wawa kepeken noka ona.
jan Akesipu li lukin e jan sewi sina lon ma anpa.
</koan>

###### Here follows a relaxed translation:

*Akesipu climbs the mountain.
  He throws the book of Pu at Tipi's feet.
  He says: "I have read it all. I am your guru equal."
  Tipi picks up the book with love and says: "Truly?"
  Akesipu says: "I will prove it to you."
  Tipi says: "Ok. But first, eat this bird with me."
  Akesipu eats the bird with Tipi.
  Then he jumps of the mountain, flapping his arms.
  Akesipu meets God at the bottom of the mountain.*
